Scuba Diving

Turtle Bay offers PADI diving under professional instructors for beginners, advanced divers and photographers. Discover pristine coral reefs inhabited by colourful marine life. Venture out to far deeper waters and explore the shark pit.

Maafushi: Resort day trip

Maafushi is a hidden gem that not many know of. The very first guesthouse for tourists was built only in the year 2009, and since then several more have mushroomed across the island. Unlike other commercialized tourist destinations, a stay at Maafushi is relaxing and rejuvenating.

Kayaking

Kayaking is a fun and rejuvenating activity to take up on your trip to Maldives. The island is extremely popular for its water sports, kayaking being one of them.You’ll find many places to rent kayaks—some with transparent bottoms—throughout the archipelago.











Kitesurfing

For a true rush of adrenalin, try your hand at kitesurfing, which combines elements of wakeboarding, windsurfing, and paragliding. Wider lagoons like those near Meedhupparu and Kuramathi are the best places for this because the water is smoother

Whale Shark Tour

The great thing about swimming with whale sharks in the Maldives is that you aren’t required to travel in a certain season to be able to enjoy this excursion. Whale sharks can actually be found in the Maldives throughout the year and they can often be found in large numbers.

Windsurfing

“It’s not the blowing of the wind, but the set of the sail” The Maldivian isles are fancy and one of the best things to do in Maldives by far.you would love to sail with the wind and enjoy the gorgeous waters with an amazing range of leisure activities.
